Over the last few generations, our lifestyles have changed radically. We now live at a faster pace and often seem to lack time for balanced, regular meals. For many, processed meals have replaced simple, nourishing wholefoods. They are convenient, but often fail to provide the nutrients we require. Since it began, Rainforest Foods’ purpose has been to help people combine good nutrition with modern living. We have sourced a range of wholefoods with exceptional nutritional richness that can support modern lifestyles rather than forcing them to change.

Man and
nature

Our Belief: Rainforest Foods believes that man and nature are forever bound together in a symbiotic relationship.
Our Purpose: Rainforest Foods exists to help man and nature take care of each other.

Shared
earth

Rainforest Foods has adopted a philosophy we call shared earth. We believe that we all share a responsibility to help protect the earth and to improve the lives of those who need a helping hand. Most of our farmers live in the developing world; by sourcing organically grown superfoods from them, we help sustain their livelihoods and allow them to continue farming in an environmentally sustainable manner. We also support a number of social partners and help fund their development work.

OUR
PARTNERS

We work with a small group of partners including certification organisations, cooperatives, producers and social partners. All of these partners play a part in helping us to provide our customers with the finest ethically sourced organic superfoods. All of our products are organically certified by the Soil Association and registered by the Vegan Society. Our social partners include our nature partner: Rainforest Concern, and our people partner: Warka Water.

Barley Grass

Barley grass is the young, leafy growth of barley and contains a broad range of vitamins and minerals.. Our barley grass is grown on the fertile Canterbury plains in New Zealand.

Find out more

Acai Berry

Acai berries are the small purple fruit produced by the acai palm, a tree found in the Amazon rainforest. It is one of the most antioxidant-rich foods on the planet.

Find out more

Cacao

Cacao is the raw base of chocolate, but it is a remarkable food in its own right. It is one of the most antioxidant-rich foods available, boasting a range of amino acids that make it a valuable part of a healthy diet.

Find out more

Chia

Chia seeds are believed to have been a staple food of the Aztecs and have been hailed as a highly valuable food for centuries. They are rich in Omega-3 and Omega-6 in what we now know to be an ideal ratio.

Find out more

Spirulina

Spirulina is a blue-green algae produced by photosynthesis. It contains a wide range of nutrients and has been described as nature’s multivitamin. It is especially high in iron.

Find out more

Maca

Maca is a Peruvian root that was prized throughout the Inca empire for its adaptogenic-like qualities that enable it to nourish and balance the body's delicate endocrine system, and to help it cope with stress.

Find out more

Chlorella

Chlorella is a useful supplement to a vegetarian or vegan diet and offers much of the nutrition we require, including protein, carbohydrates and a range of vitamins and minerals. It is especially high in vitamin B12.

Find out more

Wheatgrass

Wheatgrass is the leafy growth of the young wheat plant. It is packed with nutrients, including essential vitamins, minerals, enzymes, phytonutrients and all the essential amino acids.
